# Cross-Model Answer Basin Concept Figure

This is a conceptual figure for paper/abstract use, not a measured embedding plot. It illustrates the hypothesis that larger models may form sharper answer basins, while basin sharpness alone does not imply factual correctness. A basin becomes a factual anchor when it is supported by cross-model agreement or evidence, whereas a sharp unsupported basin can represent stable hallucination.
